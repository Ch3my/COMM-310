Tips

Always have a subject

A subject is not necesarily a person. It can be a cup, a book, a dog, anything. Why would be important to have a subject? having a subject will make your photo interesting and unique. For example, if you are photographing the Eiffel tower probably you can google for the exact photo and would no have difference. But, if instead of just the Eiffel tower in the picture you photograph a couple having a picnic with everything else as background would be more interesting and unique.

Get your time to focus

There is nothing worst than a great picture but blurred. There is a minimum of sharpeness that can be corrected with post production but a completely blurred photo is not recoverable. Personally I use autofocus in Sigle mode or continuous depending in the situation. I know some photographers that prefer use manual focus. It's okay is your own style, nevertheless take your time to focus correctly.

Look for depth in your photos

It happen to me often that I see with my eyes a great photo. I take the camera out and frame it and it's not the same. For correcting this usually add stuff in the foreground (before the subject) makes the picture feel better. Otherwise the photo looks plain and empty.
